Interesting Facts About Ratan Tata

  Born in 1937, Ratan Tata's father Naval Tata was the adopted grandson of Jamsetji Tata. His mother's name was Sooni Tata.


  Separated from his parents at the age of 10, Ratan Tata was raised by his grandmother, Navajbai Tata.


  Ratan Tata is unmarried. He has admitted that he came on the brink of marrying fourfold, but couldn't undergo it for various reasons.


  Studied architecture at Cornell Universityin 1959 and later pursued a management course at Harvard graduate school in 1975.


  Joined Tata Steel in 1961, starting from managing the furnace and shoveling limestone.


  Rejected a job offer from IBM and entered the family business, playing a key role in historic mergers.


  Ratan Tata orchestrated key mergers for his company, uniting Land Rover Jaguar with Tata Motors,Tetley with Tata Tea, and Corus with Tata   

    Steel. These strategic moves played a pivotal role in the phenomenal growth of the Tata Group.


  Conceptualized the Tata Nano in 2009, delivering the most cost-effective car in India.


  Led the Tata Group to establish a $28 million scholarship fund for Cornell University, supporting Indian undergraduate students.


  Skilled pilot and the first Indian to fly F-16 Falcon in 2007.


  Passionate about cars, Ratan Tata owns a variety of vehicles, including Ferrari California, Cadillac XLR, and more.


  Continuing the tradition at Bombay House, Ratan Tata has a kennel for stray dogs, expressing his love for canines.


  In 2010, donated $50 million for the construction of Tata Hall, an executive center for Harvard graduate school.


President Pratibha Patil awarded Ratan Tata the Padma Vibhushan in 2008 at the Rashtrapati Bhavan. Tata had previously received the Padma Bhushan in 2000. In 2021, he was honored with the Assam Baibhav for his significant contributions to cancer care in Assam.

PM Modi praises Tata Group's role in India's development, presenting the 'Assocham Enterprise of the Century Award' to Ratan Tata.

Tata Motors launched the Nano, a compact and affordable car, while also acquiring Jaguar and Land Rover.

Shantanu Naidu, a fifth-generation Tata member, developed Motopaws to protect children from accidents, creating glow-in-the-dark collars. Ratan Tata recognized the initiative, leading to a special mention and a lasting friendship. Shantanu later became Tata's business assistant.
